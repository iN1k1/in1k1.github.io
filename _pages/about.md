I am an Associate Professor of Computer Science at the University of Udine, leading research and innovation in computer vision and deep learning. I design and deploy intelligent visual systems that operate in real-world conditions, with applications spanning autonomous systems, healthcare, and large-scale industrial environments.

I have a strong track record of leadership in both academia and industry-driven research, serving as Principal Investigator and Co-PI on international projects, including EU and U.S. Office of Naval Research initiatives. My work consistently bridges cutting-edge research with practical impact, translating advanced AI models into deployable solutions.

I previously held a tenure-track Assistant Professor position and worked as a Visiting Research Scientist at the Video Computing Group at the University of California, Riverside, under Amit K. Roy-Chowdhury, focusing on scalable visual recognition and learning systems.

My background combines two PhDs—one in Computer Vision and one in Information Engineering—complemented by postdoctoral research on advanced learning architectures. This dual foundation enables me to approach AI systems from both theoretical and system-level perspectives, driving innovation from concept to deployment.

## Research Interests

- Computer Vision and Deep Learning
- Intelligent Visual Systems
- Autonomous Systems
- Healthcare Applications
- Large-scale Industrial AI
- Scalable Visual Recognition
- Learning Architectures
